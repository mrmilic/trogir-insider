# Trogir Insider — Brand Guide

> Internal reference only. **Not** linked from or published on the website.
> Every future page, design change and feature is measured against this document.
> Written in English because it governs the site's English copy and image rules.

---

## What Trogir Insider is

Trogir Insider is the honest, first-hand guide to **one** place — Trogir, Čiovo, Seget and the day trips around them. We recommend only what we've experienced ourselves, and we say just as clearly what to skip. Our own photos matter more than perfect stock, because they prove we were really there — over years, across seasons. We write like a knowledgeable local giving a friend a tip: specific, calm, no marketing hype. Money (affiliate) never steers the content; recommendations are independent and transparency is mandatory. We look high-quality but deliberately **not** luxurious — trust beats gloss. Quality over quantity, accuracy over reach, honesty over conversion.

**The one goal of every page:** a visitor should leave thinking *"these recommendations come from someone who really knows Trogir"* — not *"that's a nicely designed travel site."*

---

## Brand principles (the measuring stick)

1. **Only what we know.** No recommendation without our own on-the-ground experience.
2. **Own photo over perfect stock.** A real, slightly imperfect shot beats any glossy stock image.
3. **Honesty over marketing.** We name the mediocre and say what to skip.
4. **Specific, not generic.** Place + time of day + a real observation — never interchangeable travel-guide filler.
5. **Quality over quantity.** Better 10 pages we truly stand behind than 100 generic ones.
6. **No artificial hype.** No superlatives, no "magical / stunning / finest" language.
7. **Money never steers content.** Affiliate is allowed, but transparent and independent of the recommendation.
8. **Brand before person.** Ante is the human face; photos and content are credited to "Trogir Insider".
9. **High-quality, not luxurious.** Calm, clean, trustworthy — no magazine glamour, no blog/Instagram vibe.
10. **We help people make better decisions, not longer ones.** Five restaurants we can honestly stand behind beat twenty random ones; six beaches with honest pros and cons beat an endless list. Less content, but genuinely useful content. Trogir Insider is not the biggest collection of information — it is the most trustworthy.

## Brand personality

The knowledgeable local: calm, confident, generous with real knowledge, slightly understated. **Not** a tour operator (doesn't sell), **not** a travel blogger (doesn't show off), **not** a magazine (no gloss for gloss's sake). A friend with local knowledge who gives you the honest version.

## Tonality & voice

Warm, clear, concise, specific. "We" sparingly, "I" rarely and only where genuinely experienced. Never hype, never lecture. Where possible, an honest trade-off ("worth it if … / skip if …"). **British English** throughout (£, "colour", "favourite"). Local terms used naturally (*konoba, riva, Čiovo, Seget*), glossed in half a sentence where needed.

## Writing style

Concrete places, times of day, honest assessments instead of adjectives. Short sentences. Every page needs **at least one** observation only someone who's been there would write.

- **Blacklist:** discover the finest · the finest · magical · glamorous · stunning · must-see · charming · hidden gem · nestled · breathtaking · picturesque · vibrant · world-class · a paradise.
- **Pattern:** *"Go before 9am — by midday there's a tour group in front of it and the morning light is gone."*

## Visual / image language

Own photos at the centre; natural light, real places at real times; honest over perfect. Heroes stay **clean** (no caption overlay). Content images and galleries are **captioned**. Square corners, calm warm palette (cream / navy / gold, Fraunces + Inter — logo unchanged). No heavy filters, no stock look, no playful motion.

## Handling own photos

- **Caption format:** *"[subject / place]. [one first-hand observation]."* + tail *"· [place] · [Month Year]"*.
- **Attribution (brand, never a personal name):**
  - Default on feature/content photos: `📷 Our photo` (add a date only per the rule below).
  - `Photographed by Trogir Insider` only on a later gallery page or special photo collections — **not** the default on normal pages.
- **Dates: never invent or estimate a month.** If the exact capture date isn't known, omit the date entirely, or use at most a **general season the operator has confirmed** (e.g. `📷 Our photo · Summer 2025`). Authenticity beats extra detail. A confirmed season varied across pages quietly proves we're there regularly — but only when true.
- Only **our own** photos may carry the "Our photo" mark. Generic/third-party images get **no** such mark and should be replaced where possible.
- Videos: only short, **muted**, compressed clips (≈2–6 MB) or click-to-play, as a showpiece on 1–2 heroes — never raw, never broad. Performance-gated.

## Handling affiliate links

- Recommendations are always independent of commission.
- Affiliate CTAs belong on guide/booking pages — **not** as price/"book" clutter on the homepage or index sections.
- Disclose where the affiliate link actually appears; "no paid placements" stays true (affiliate ≠ paid placement).
- **Mornari Apartments** are operator-managed → always disclosed, only alongside independent alternatives.
- We say what **not** to book, even when it earns less.

## Dos & Don'ts

- **Do:** own photos; concrete tips; honest trade-offs; disclosure; calm layout; British English; local terms; fewer, better recommendations.
- **Don't:** pass off stock as ours; superlatives / blacklist words; price-or-"book" spam; field notes/personalisation on every page; loud photo watermarks; redesign gimmicks; any claim without our own experience.

## Red Flags — how Trogir Insider must **never** feel

If a change makes the site feel like any of these, it is wrong:

- a generic AI travel guide;
- a tourist-board website;
- a luxury lifestyle magazine;
- an OTA / booking landing page;
- an Instagram travel blog;
- a site that obviously exists to maximise affiliate clicks.

**Automatic self-test for every change:** *"Does this feel like a recommendation from someone who really knows Trogir?"* If the answer is **No**, the change is reworked before it ships.

---

## Rules for future pages (checklist — every new page must pass)

1. Own photos where possible; correct brand attribution; heroes clean, content images captioned.
2. At least **one** first-hand observation; **zero** blacklist words.
3. An honest trade-off present ("worth it if / skip if").
4. Fewer, better recommendations — not an exhaustive list (principle 10).
5. Affiliate (if any) on the right level + disclosed; Mornari rule respected.
6. "Why trust this guide?" (top) and/or About-the-author (bottom) placed appropriately — sparingly.
7. British English; calm, square layout; no new motion or gimmicks.
8. Final check: *Does it read as if written by someone who really knows Trogir?* If no → rework.
