Plan the practical bits

The good news: Split Airport sits right next to Trogir, so you can be in the old town minutes after landing. Here's everything else worth knowing before you go.

Arriving

Getting to Trogir

Trogir is one of the easiest places on the coast to reach — the airport is almost on its doorstep.

From Split Airport (SPU)

Just a few kilometres away — a 5–10 minute taxi or transfer. The cheapest option is the airport shuttle bus toward Trogir.

From Split city

City bus line 37 runs frequently between Split and Trogir via the airport. In summer, a seasonal catamaran also links the two harbours.

By car

Trogir is just off the main coastal road and the A1 motorway. Easy to reach, but leave the car outside the old town (see parking). Compare car rental at Split Airport.

Good to know

Before you arrive

01

No cars in the old town

The historic island is car-free. Park on the mainland side or on Čiovo and walk in over the bridge — it's only a few minutes across.

02

The currency is the euro

Croatia uses the euro. Cards are widely accepted, but carry some cash for markets, small konobas and parking machines. Tipping around 10% is normal for good service.

03

Best time to visit

May, June and September bring warm sea, lighter crowds and better prices. July and August are hot and busy — book early and start sightseeing before mid-morning. See our best time to visit Trogir guide.

04

Getting around

The old town is tiny and entirely walkable. For the beaches and Čiovo coves, use local buses, a taxi, or hire a car or scooter for the day.

FAQ

Trogir travel info — common questions

How do you get from Split Airport to Trogir?

Split Airport (SPU) is only 5–10 minutes from Trogir. Options are a private transfer, a taxi, the local bus (line 37) or a hire car — see our Split Airport to Trogir guide, or compare a hire car at Split Airport.

Do you need a car in Trogir?

Not for the car-free old town or for organised day trips. A hire car helps for the quiet Čiovo coves and self-driving day trips — easy to compare and collect at the airport.

What is the currency in Trogir, Croatia?

Croatia uses the euro. Cards are widely accepted, but carry some cash for markets, small konobas, parking machines and bus tickets.

How do you get to the islands from Trogir?

By boat — organised tours and private charters run to the Blue Lagoon, Šolta and beyond, and a summer catamaran links Trogir and Split harbours. See our Trogir boat tours guide, or pick a base in our best hotels in Trogir guide.
