Trogir with Kids: A Family Guide

Trogir is more family-friendly than it first looks: calm beaches, short transfers and a walkable old town. Here's where to stay, the gentlest beaches, easy day trips and the practical tips that keep a hot afternoon happy.

Beyond the beach

Easy adventures kids enjoy

Keep outings short and low-effort. A little goes a long way in the heat.

Climb the cathedral bell tower

A quick climb with a big reward — rooftop views over the whole island. Pair it with a wander around the fortress, which kids love to explore. More on the sights guide.

A gentle boat trip to the Blue Lagoon

Calm, shallow, brilliantly clear water that's ideal for a first snorkel. Half-day trips keep it manageable — see our Blue Lagoon tour guide.

Krka waterfalls as a day trip

The closest big day trip (about an hour) with boardwalks kids can manage and the chance of a swim. Details on the Krka from Trogir page.

Ice cream on the Riva

The evening passeggiata is made for families — a slow lap of the waterfront, an ice cream and space to run around once the day cools.

Practical tips

Keeping it stress-free

01

Beat the midday heat

Sightsee early, retreat to the pool or a shaded beach at midday, and come back out in the evening. The old town bakes at noon in summer.

02

Pack water shoes

Most beaches are pebble or stony at the entry. Cheap water shoes save tears — bring a pair per child.

03

Consider a hire car

For the quieter coves, supermarket runs and flexible day trips, a car makes family life easier. Compare hire cars at the airport.

04

Book early for summer

Family rooms and apartments with pools sell out first for July and August — reserve by spring, ideally with free cancellation.

FAQ

Trogir with kids — common questions

Is Trogir good for families with kids?

Yes. Trogir is compact, walkable and car-free in the old town, with calm pebble beaches nearby, short transfers from the airport and easy boat trips. Family-friendly resorts in Seget and on Čiovo make it a low-stress base on the Dalmatian coast.

Which beach near Trogir is best for children?

Medena Beach in Seget is the easiest family choice — a long, gently shelving pebble beach with shade, cafés and facilities. Pantan, by a nature reserve, has the shallowest, partly sandy water and is great for little ones.

Where should families stay in Trogir?

Resorts and apartments in Seget or on Čiovo work best for families: pools, parking, calmer beaches and more space, with the old town a short bus, boat or drive away. Old-town stays are charming but have stairs, no parking and fewer family facilities.

What can you do with kids in Trogir besides the beach?

Climb the cathedral bell tower and explore Kamerlengo Fortress, take a gentle boat trip to the Blue Lagoon to snorkel, get ice cream on the Riva, and visit Krka waterfalls as an easy day trip. Most are short and low-effort for small legs.
